What is Early Childhood Development?

Early Childhood Development (ECD) refers to the physical, cognitive, emotional, and social growth of children from birth to age 8. It is a critical period that shapes future learning, behavior, and health. This foundational stage is when children build the core capabilities for lifelong success. - Sourced from WHO/UNICEF guidance
